Harmony in Ancient Echoes: Rudra Veena

  • About Myself

    Introducing Dr. Jayant Kumar Das, a maestro proficient in three extraordinary instruments - the Sitar, Surbahar, and Rudra Veena. He has earned the highest accolades, receiving an 'A' grade from All India Radio for his mastery of all three instruments.

    Surbahar and Rudra Veena, both rare gems in the realm of Indian classical music, are cherished by only a handful of artists who are dedicated to preserving the timeless tradition of Dhrupad style, a tradition rooted in the ancient Sam Veda.

  • Experiences

    Dr. Das holds a doctorate in music and has undertaken extensive research in both Hindusthani and Karnatik music, particularly focusing on the instrumental aspects of these musical traditions. His contributions extend beyond his musical prowess.

    He has shared his knowledge as a lecturer at various universities in the United States, delivering enlightening lectures and enthralling performances. As a cultural delegate of the Government of India, he has represented his nation with pride in the United States and Europe.

  • About Myself

    Awards and honors have adorned his illustrious career, recognizing his extraordinary contributions to the world of music. Dr. Das continues his tireless efforts to ensure the continuity of the Dhrupad tradition through his Gurukul in Bangalore, where he imparts his knowledge and expertise on Rudra Veena and Surbahar, following the sacred Guru-Shishya Parampara.

    Furthermore, he carries the legacy of the Etawah Gharana in Sitar, a tradition rooted in Gayaki style within the Kheyal Ang, demonstrating his unwavering commitment to preserving the rich tapestry of Indian classical music. Dr. Jayant Kumar Das stands as a beacon of artistry, culture, and tradition, enriching the world with his musical brilliance.
